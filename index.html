Why this exists

Three gaps stand between you and good skin.

Not one of them is solved by another product. One is not knowing where to start. One is not doing what you already know. One is not keeping it going — money leaking into gimmicks, staples quietly running out. Everyone lands in at least one.

The knowing gap

"Where do I even start?"

You'd like to take care of your skin, but the aisle is overwhelming, the jargon is a wall, and asking someone feels embarrassing. So you do nothing — or buy whatever the ad said.

The doing gap

"I own it all. I still skip it."

The shelf is stocked and the plan lives in your head — it just keeps not happening on your face. Week one is easy; week six is where routines go to die.

The keeping gap

"Why did I buy that?"

The $200 miracle cream that did what the $30 one does. The cleanser that ran out mid-week. Money goes to marketing, not ingredients — and the things that work go unstocked.

One app closes all three. It has two ideas in it, and we named it after both: the Stack — what you own — and the Loop — what keeps it working.

Pillar one

The Stack is yours. Not a template.

Your stack is your products, in your order, on your schedule — built around your skin, your hair, your week, your budget. Not a dermatologist's ideal. Not an influencer's shelf. Yours.

Starting from nothing? The Stack starts at zero — a first stack can be three products and five minutes a day. Shelf already full? Bring it all in — skincare, hair, scent rotation, supplements — as ordered, step-by-step routines that react when your life changes.

And when you're deciding what earns a spot on the shelf next, you get advice in context — grounded in what you already own and what you're trying to do — so the next addition makes your stack better, not just bigger.

Pillar two

Loop closes the loop.

Loop is the intelligence inside The Stack — it knows your shelf, your skin, your budget and your history. Its job is in the name: find the open loops between you and good skin, and close them. Four ways:

No dumb questions

The questions you'd never ask out loud — "do I actually need toner?", "what order do these go in?", "what even is my skin type?" — Loop answers privately, patiently, from absolute zero. No counter staff. No judgment. No one watching you learn.

Advice becomes action

Answers don't stop at words — they land as changes in tonight's routine. And when life squeezes you, Loop adapts the routine instead of letting it collapse, so week six looks like week one.

Ingredients over influencers

Loop reads the ingredients list, not the ad. It matches actives to your skin and your budget — and when the $30 product does what the $200 one does, it says so. Fewer products, chosen better.

Never run dry

The Stack tracks what's on the shelf and how fast you go through it. Loop flags the restock before the bottle's empty — and stops you buying a third of something you already own two of.

Loop never ranks by commission.

When Loop links to a product, we may earn a small commission — the same small commission whether it's the $30 answer or the $200 one. Recommendations are ranked by ingredients, fit and price. Only that. So we just tell you the truth.

Questions

The short answers.

What's "the Stack" and what's "the Loop"?

The Stack is your side: your products, organised into ordered routines built around you. The Loop is the app's side: the intelligence that keeps you and that routine connected — turning advice into action and intentions into a habit that lasts. You build the Stack; Loop keeps it closed.

I know nothing about skincare. Is this for me?

Especially for you. Loop is built for the questions people are embarrassed to ask anywhere else — it starts from zero, explains without jargon, and builds you a simple first stack you can actually keep. Nobody's watching you learn.

How does The Stack make money from recommendations?

Some product links earn us a small commission — roughly the same whether the product costs $30 or $200. Loop's recommendations are ranked by ingredients, fit for your skin, and your budget. Never by what pays us more. If the cheap one works, Loop says so.

Is the free plan actually usable?

Yes — it's the complete stack engine: unlimited daily tracking, the step-by-step runner and all themes. Your data stays on your device. Paid plans add cloud sync and Loop.

What's the difference between Standard and Professional Loop?

On Standard, Loop responds when you ask. On Professional, Loop also works unprompted — noticing patterns, suggesting adjustments and checking in when your behaviour changes. Same brain, different level of initiative.

Where is my data stored?

Free plan data never leaves your device. On paid plans, data syncs through secure cloud infrastructure hosted in Australia, isolated per account. You can export or delete it at any time.

Does Loop give medical or dermatological advice?

No. Loop helps you run the routine you've chosen — ordering, scheduling, consistency. For skin conditions or reactions, see a professional.

Which platforms are supported?

Native apps for phone and tablet on iOS and Android, plus a website for desktop — all sharing the same account and sync.
